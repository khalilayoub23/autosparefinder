# Dropship-to-Israel Brand Analysis (2026-07-12)

Analysis of the aftermarket brand list against **our terms for dropshipping to Israel**,
tiered by sourcing economics (measured this session):
- **Local IL distributor** → buy in-country, ~₪29 domestic shipping, no customs delay = **best**.
- **US-only** → RockAuto-style freight-forwarder, ~$58/₪175 per-order shipping floor = only viable for **high-value parts**.
- Reminder: our **biggest supplier car-parts.ie does NOT ship to Israel** (EU-only) — so the EU-sourced brand inventory we already hold needs an IL buy-point to be fulfillable without EU forwarding.

## Strategic conclusion — two distinct plays

**Priority A — OEM-grade brands we ALREADY stock in volume, but EU-sourced (car-parts.ie).**
Bosch, Valeo, Delphi, DENSO, NGK, Monroe, Aisin, KYB, TYC, Continental. We already list
tens of thousands of these SKUs, mostly priced — but they're EU-origin, so fulfillment to
IL currently depends on the (non-existent) car-parts.ie→IL path. **Onboarding the local
Israeli distributor** (DENSO Israel, Valeo via Comet & Progress, Monroe via EFS, NGK IL rep)
converts this existing inventory from "needs EU forwarding" to "domestic-fulfillable" AND
fills the unpriced subset (Continental 801, Bosch 795, Valeo 365, DENSO 182…). **Highest ROI**
— it leverages inventory we already have and solves the shipping gap.

**Priority B — niche performance / 4x4 / audio brands we DON'T stock.**
Borla, MagnaFlow, Remus, Fox, ARB, Alpine, Rockford Fosgate, Meguiar's, Mickey Thompson.
Near-zero catalog presence → onboarding their IL distributors ADDS exclusive, high-margin
SKUs. Lower volume, but differentiated. Several have direct contacts already
(Borla→RADAD/Yoram Duan +972-50-528-0417; Fox→Outback logistics@outback.co.il; ARB→Henfeld;
Remus→PIT-STOP; MagnaFlow→Kogol Unitrade).
